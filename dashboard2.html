2. Contributions To Total Attributable Behavioural Risk Dashboard

This dashboard is about five purchase-to-consumption processes as model-systems:

  • Eggs
  • Bivalve molluscs
  • Ready-to-eat (RTE) foods
  • Chicken
  • Cross-cutting issues related to general hygiene and kitchen equipment
Each purchase-to-consumption process can be understood as a user journey that consists of different stages in a logical order (preference and planning, shopping, storing, unpacking, food washing, etc.). The chart below shows the contributions to total attributable behavioural risk, cross-classified in terms of scope (the four model foods and cross-cutting issues) and divided into user journey stages which respresent the purchase-to-consumption process. So for example, about 28% of total attributable behavioural risk was associated with cross-cutting issues such as kitchen equipment (including refrigerator use and temperature) and general hygiene.
