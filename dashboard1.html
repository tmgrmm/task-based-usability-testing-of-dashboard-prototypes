Task-based usability testing of dashboard prototypes

1. Relative Risk Dashboard

The adverse event addressed in the present assessment is the occurrence of an acute gastroenteritis episodein a household in the population defined by the ten EU and EEA countries In the following dashboard the risk will be assessed in terms of the below listed standard epidemiological risk assessment endpoints:

  • Prevalence: Household prevalence of gastroenteritis in %
  • Incidence: 12-month incidence rate
  • Health Burden: Disability-adjusted life years lost (DALY)
  • Productivity Lost: Workdays lost per household
  • Healthcare Demand: Household demand for healthcare services in %
The dashboard is splitted into observed risk and attributable beavioural risk. For giving an example, the following aggregated observation could be made for the ten countries through a survey:

  • Prevalence: 39% of households reported at least one gastroenteritits episode in the past year
  • Incidence: 0.55 gastroenteritis episodes per person-year-at-risk
  • Health Burden: 0.15 DALY per 1000 population
  • Productivity Lost: 0.62 workdays lost per household
  • Healthcare Demand: 9.8% of households reported medical treatment for gastroenteritis
The values attributable to the behavioral risk are estimated and provide information on how food handling behavior of individual households can influence the observed risk.

Vulnerable Groups: Relative risk is estimated for vulnerable sub-populations, including households with members older than 65 years, pregnant women, immunocompromised individuals, persons between 16 and 18 years living alone or in shared housing, persons between 19 and 25 years living alone or in shared housing, and men below 30 living alone or in shared housing.

Maps: The spatial distributions of observed risk and risk attributable to behavioural factors over the NUTS2 regions of ten EU and EEA member states (Denmark, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Norway, Portugal, Romania, Spain, United Kingdom) are characterised.

Task 1: Which vulnerable group has the highest healthcare demand? And how much of it is attributable to behavioural risk?

Task 2: In which greater region are the most workdays lost per household (productivity loss) attributable to behavioural factors? And what is the observed workdays lost in total for this region?
